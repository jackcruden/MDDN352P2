Weather Lad Your Intelligent Weather Companion

Ever wanted your weather app to tell you relevent information instead of contextless numbers and irrelevant graphics? Weather Lad caters to you and provides a customised experience, learning your preferences over time.

Will I need a jacket today?
Yea, temperatures will get down to 14 later in the day, so you'll probably want a jacket. Morning looks warm though!
Awesome, cheers!
Conversational

For a pesonalised experience users can teach Weather Lad their nickname and location. Once learnt, Weather Lad will use the user's location data to provide accurate weather information.

Over time Weather Lad will learn the user's common queries and begin providing contextualised information without prompt.

Hey, I don't believe we've met before. I'm Weather Lad, what can I call you?
Hi Weather Lad, I'm Jack.
Kia ora, Jack. Looks like you're in Kelburn, Wellington. Does that sound right?
Yep.

Sweet as. What about later this week?
Looks like Friday after work at Lyall Bay could be good, warm too at 24. Check again on Friday morning.
Contextual

Weather data has typically never been provided with any context, or relevance to the user. Weather Lad provides relevant information and recommendations alongside accurate weather data.

Will I need a raincoat today?
Nope! It's gonna be sunny all day today. The high will be 23 around 1pm.
So warm! Too windy for a swim?
Nope! It's a bit windy this morning, but it should die down around midday. Hit up Oriental Bay.
Accessible

As a standalone website Weather Lad can be viewed on any smartphone, tablet or desktop. Optimised for speed and ease of use Weather Lad is always there when you need it.

Accessibility

Weather Lad will be designed to be used on mobile, tablet and desktop screens with different considerations in mind. The mobile layout will prioritise the conversational aspect with the input box being the focal point. Whereas with the desktop layout the focus will be on the display of weather data. The desktop layout will likely most commonly be used as a screensaver.

Sensors

The GPS sensor of the phone, tablet or laptop viewing the website will be used to determine the user's location. No other sensors are really necessary for the operation of the website. The accelerometer and/or compass data could be used for enhancing the website aesthetically. However, they will not really enhance the website technically.

Challenges

The most obvious difficulty is getting Weather Lad to understand what the user is saying. I have a couple of ideas as to how this could work. The most achievable method I feel is to have a set of words that Weather Lad will recognise. For example, it will need to understand a set of weather words (rain, cloud, sun, etc), a set of clothing words (jacket, t-shirt, jersey, etc), and a bunch of others. Each message will be scanned for the aforementioned set of words.
